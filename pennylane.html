Penny Lane - The Beatles

01.24.17




Today I was having an average Tuesday at work. Late in the afternoon, I checked my makeshift office mailbox. In it was an envelope with my first name on it and enough girth to know that it was more than one page. Uh-oh...

At first thought, I imagined that it might be a complaint from a member or a letter of resignation. How’s that for optimism???

Upon opening it, I found an extensive list of Beatles' songs and a hand written note of three short sentences that read: “205 songs. Almost 7 months of writing. You did not mention “Penny Lane” in your top 5 Beatles songs.” Hmmmm...

Last week, I was having a conversation with my friend Bob (from early AM yoga) about the Beatles. I shared with him of my pursuit of a daily song blog. I told Bob about the previous night when my son had asked me which was my favorite Fab 4 song (see Song #19 post). As I perused this issue with Bob, I was still pondering my answer. We jointly rattled off a number of songs which we thought could earn top honor. Incidentally, Bob (like my hubby) is a big George fan (gotta love the slide guitar).

The note (of girth) today was from Bob. How could I have possibly forgotten the song that celebrates my own name? I’ve spent the night humming the song about the street where Lennon and McCartney caught the bus as children. "Penny Lane" is a real street in Liverpool and is quite famous to this day.

This song was undoubtedly my favorite Beatles tune as a little girl. It made me feel special hearing my name sung out by the world’s most popular band. My brother and sisters had the album and made it a staple in their regular rotation in the basement family room of our childhood house.

Penny Lane paints the picture of city street on a beautiful day. There is nothing not to like about it. Especially the title, lol! Thanks Bob for the reminder!

“Penny Lane is in my ears and in my eyes There beneath the blue suburban skies I sit and meanwhile back..”
