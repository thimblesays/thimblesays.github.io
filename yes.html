I've seen all good people a) your move b) all good people - Yes

01.02.17

This song is interesting, both musically and lyrically. Throughout the years, I've remained curious about the meaning of this song. Though "I've Seen All Good People" uses the game of chess as a metaphor, I have always believed that the song highlights the importance of being an individual while still "playing by the rules". It is important to notice and respect others' boundaries, but it is also important to deeper explore their thoughts and intentions. We must not live solely in our own head.

I love how it moves the listener from something soft (a) to something a little bolder (b). Not intensely bolder, but just enough to grasp the listener's attention. The lyric, "I've seen all good people turn their heads each day, so satisfied I'm on my way" makes me envision a city street with strangers in hats, nodding accolades then carrying on. It is important to acknowledge our fellow humans, even if they are simply passerbys. Maybe, a nod turns into a conversation. A conversation into a friendship. A friendship into a bond.
