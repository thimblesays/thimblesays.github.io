Old Man - Neil Young

01.01.17

This song has always spoken to me. Ever since college, I have been a huge Neil Young fan. Accordingly, I have acquired a substantial amount of vinyl sporting his name.

I found Young's "Old Man" particularly intriguing because he wrote and produced the song when he was in his mere 20s. Young's insight at such a young age is pretty damn cool. Contrarily, my 20s was plagued with self involvement. I enjoyed being surrounded by people my own age and discussing issues that I believed only my fellow generational friends could understand (#SELFCENTERED).

Young's song title itself, "Old Man", crosses the generational divide. The song's simple yet most known phrase, "Old man look at my life, I'm a lot like you", immediately drew my attention towards my father. Although, I know that was not the intent of this verse.

"Old Man" is a comfort song for me. It is simultaneously sweet, introspective, and humble. While Neil Young has an exhaustive library of music, this is certainly the song with which I want to begin.
